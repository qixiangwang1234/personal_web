STAIRS: Staircase-based Temporal Analytic for Inferring Rich Stories

项目摘要 / Project Summary

本文提出了一种基于楼梯磨损的时空分析方法，旨在通过低成本、非破坏性测量手段和数学模型，从楼梯的磨损中挖掘历史使用信息。研究基于Archard磨损定律，结合经典力学和正态分布理论，建立了分阶段磨损模型，并通过离散化计算和遗传算法解决了多任务问题，包括使用频率估计、上下楼比例分析、同时使用人数推断、材料匹配度评估等。针对不同材质（石材与木材），分别设计了疲劳失效模型和热软化模型以分析交通模式（稳态或脉冲式）。实验验证表明，模型具有较高的稳定性和可靠性，为考古学提供了实用工具。

This paper proposes a staircase wear-based spatiotemporal analysis method to extract historical usage information through low-cost, non-destructive measurements and mathematical models. Based on the Archard wear law, classical mechanics, and normal distribution theory, we established a phased wear model. Discrete calculations and genetic algorithms were employed to solve multi-task problems, including estimating usage frequency, analyzing ascending/descending ratios, inferring simultaneous user counts, and evaluating material compatibility. For different materials (stone and wood), fatigue failure and thermal softening models were designed to analyze traffic patterns (steady or pulsing). Experimental verification demonstrated high model stability and reliability, providing practical tools for archaeology. Keywords: Archard wear law, discretized calculation, genetic algorithms, fatigue failure.
